Summary

The IoT world is made up of thousand smart objects (smartphone, tablet, wireless headphones, sensors, actuators...). These objects are equipped with positioning and communication technologies and are integrated into a communication network so they can enter, store and process data and interact with other objects, systems or people. They can be embedded or fixed in other objects and capture data about position and sensors, as well as execute decision and control functions.

This training unit is divided into several modules:

  • Protocol for wireless sensor network
  • From 3G to 5G
  • Energy for connected objects
  • Security in networks of connected objects

Protocol and Communication

Protocol for wireless sensor network

Research assignment

Nowadays, there are a large number of existing networks protocol for IoT devices, some of them are: LoRA, Sigfox, BLE, ZigBee, NB-IoT and M2M. Their characteristics differ and it is therefore very important to choose the one which to use in order to best meet the requirements.

By doing research assignment (include written report and oral presentation), i was albe to compare these protocols by different aspects like physical layer, security, cost, energy-efficiency... For me, i chose to focus on the Narrow-Band IoT (Nb-IoT) based on 5G where i explained the characteristics of its physical layer, its operation mode, its frequency modulation, its power consumption, and also its security.

Software defined radio lab

Software Defined Radio (SDR) was second part of this module, i was able to follow serial labs that focused on SDR and Universal Software Radio Peripheral (USRP). These labs were an opportunity to apply the concepts of signal modulation / demodulation. After seeing the mathematical aspects of signal modulation and studying how a USRP works, we analyzed the different operations that make up the signal acquisition chain. Then, in a second, I was able to get started with GNURadio in order to digitally process radio signals so that they could be analyzed and modified for certain usecases. By combining the use of GNURadio and USRP, I was then able to acquire and process signals from the High Frequency (HF) and Very High Frequency (VHF) bands in order to be able to listen to FM radio transmissions as well as intercontinental communications of meteorological information for airplanes (VOLMET).

From 3G to 5G

During the "3G to 5G" module, i was able to discuss about the evolution of the Global System for Mobile Communications (GSM) network from 2G to 5G. I also worked on a presentation of the emergence of 5G, its characteristics and its development in France and in Toulouse. I focused my analysis of 5G in IoT context, how it can promote smart cities by connecting sensor networks that would manage traffic and quickly determine streetlight, how it enables better remote surgery, how it may connect self-driving cars and how it affects the factory of future... Beside that, i also had a chance to discuss about the health, political and moral aspect of 5G especially in France. Below is my presentation for more details.

Energy for connected objects

During my final semester, this module consisted of theoretical courses. These have enabled me to consolidate my skills in the choice of power sources when building on-board systems, in particular by answering the questions about how to power an on-board system; how to choose the type of battery best suited to the needs of my system; or what are the main methods of energy recovery and how are they implement.

As i am a student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department of INSA Toulouse, i already had a chance to attend the lecture of the main concept behind Energy of connected objects in my 4th year. Thus, i was able to see what are the main characteristics of the different rechargeable or non rechareable batteries, in order to be able to choose the best component according to the use case. The possible dangers of using certain batteries depending on the conditions and context of use were also discussed. This module was evaluated during a written exam.
