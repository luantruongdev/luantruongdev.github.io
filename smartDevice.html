Smart Devices

AIME internship

AIME (L'Atelier Interuniversitaire de Micro-nano Électronique) is a technological platform accessible to students, universities and industrialists. It's equipped with new technological equipement needed for conception, production and test of silicon integrated circuits. During my 3 days intership at AIME, i was able to get familiar to the whole process of sensor fabrication.

“ Coming from computer science, almost all of this course was a discovery for me. I did very little electronics during my curriculum (coming from a computer science DUT) and this module being very much focused on this discipline, I was able to discover and rediscover many methods and concepts. I found extremely interesting the discovery of the manufacture of a sensor and it makes us take another look at these sensors, especially on the fact of having to optimize the manufacture of these sensors and all the reflection behind it. The integration part with a shield was also very interesting, the only approach I had of the arduino before that was the lighting of leds, seeing how to integrate something that has been manufactured was particularly interesting. The communication part, even if it was less innovative, was also very interesting and allowed me to see how to use an already established architecture (TTN) and connect it with a sensor we had made. Finally, the writing of the datasheet allows us to report on what we have done and to take a step back to know the useful information at the industrial level. ”

Summary

This project is about the fabrication from A to Z of a gas sensor and its deployment in an IOT context.

To achieve the main goal of this project, each of these steps need to be realized:

  • AIME laboratory for sensor creation
  • PCB circuit design for sensor integration
  • Software development for the sensor's particular use case.
